    My favorite projects so far? Conducting research on the associations between parents’ parenting style and adult depression and anxiety at UC Berkeley. I recruited international participants and utilized an online survey through Qualtrics to assess the various parenting styles and both depression and anxiety symptoms. I gathered information on demographics, used the Parenting Style Questionnaire to measure the parents’ parenting style, The Zung Self-Rating Depression Scale (SDS) to measure depression, and the 7-Item Generalized Anxiety Disorder Scale to evaluate anxiety symptoms. Then I conducted a correlation test, constructed bivariate linear models to analyze the effects of parenting style on adult depression and anxiety, and used multiple variable regressions to further my understanding. Finally, I visualized my findings.

    Results:

    - Authoritative parenting predicts less adult depression.

    - There is no significant relationship between parenting style and adult anxiety.

    (R was used for this project.)
