    • Questions and Answers

    My favorite projects so far? Conducting research on the associations between parents’ parenting style and adult depression and anxiety at UC Berkeley. I recruited international participants and utilized an online survey through Qualtrics to assess the various parenting styles and both depression and anxiety symptoms. I gathered information on demographics, used the Parenting Style Questionnaire to measure the parents’ parenting style, The Zung Self-Rating Depression Scale (SDS) to measure depression, and the 7-Item Generalized Anxiety Disorder Scale to evaluate anxiety symptoms. Then I conducted a correlation test on anxiety and depression levels, constructed bivariate linear models to analyze the effects of parenting style on adult depression and anxiety, and used multiple variable regressions to further my understanding. Finally, I visualized my findings.

    Results:

    - Authoritative parenting predicts less depression.

    - There is no significant correlation between parenting style and adult anxiety. However, there is a non-significant negative correlation between adult anxiety and the authoritative parenting style.

    (R was used for this project.)
